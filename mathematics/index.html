Whiteboards and Brown Paper

In a tour of my room, you'd see a side of myself I hide from nobody. Prototype electroscopes, a (surprisingly-constructionally-sound) cardboard chair, two whiteboards, countless math books—you get the jist.

But, my favorite decorations are taped to the walls: hand-crafted posters of my favorite math problems, all made with brown paper. They're an homage to Numberphile, a YouTube channel comporised of mini-documentaries about famous problems and mathematicians.

That's not what makes them special to me, though; rather, it's the message of passion, of perspective.

The creator of the channel—Brady Haran—isn't a mathematician, though. He's a journalist. As such, his perspective is wholly different; he carries a different vibe throughout his videos. Each one is carefully (yet, authentically) crafted by math-lovers, for math-lovers. Touring the world, Haran interviews the most passionate mathematicians. He introduces the humanity behind the numbers: a conglomeration of lived experiences—a bridge between those who solved the problems and those who admire the beauty of the proof.

So, my brown papers represent some of my stories. Math is the subject at the core of my ambitions: a vehicle for everything that I love. These papers mean much more than the numbers scrawled on them with sharpie; they share me, my experiences, my ambitions.
