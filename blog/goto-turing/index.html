City Pop and Interstate Driving

I've been on a city pop craze recently.

Jason sent me a few Anri albums a while ago, and I mean, yeah, she's great and I love her music. But, there's something especially refreshing about rolling down the window—barrelling down the interstate, toward the future, with the slick, nostalgic jazz beat humming through my ears. My arm stuck out and wrapped up onto the roof; the conglomeration of (what can only be described as) truck smell and a melting chocolate Payday bar 1 pervaded my half of the car. It was in this peculiar state that I vibed out to Timely!!

I wouldn't have wanted it any other way, though. In any case, city pop isn't why I'm writing this.

The Big Bang

Last weekend, I visited UT Austin for >goto_Turing, and it was pretty cool.

Friday morning, we drove into Austin from Jonestown (where my aunt and uncle live), and stayed in a hotel on the east side of I-35. Suffice it to say, it took all but five minutes—we arrived at the hotel, and, almost instantly, I asked my dad to take me to UT. I got in a little bit of nerdy pregaming 2, so it was a nice slow-paced day before goto.

Overall, it was a great weekend filled with lots of great memories—morning walks down Speedway, interesting faculty lectures 3, dining at Jester and bonding over the bad food 4, throwing gutterballs in the Union basement... I could go on.

Taking the time to slow down and enjoy the moment is something that I've cherished through this experience. After all, it's the beginning of a big shift in my life; I've lived in Frisco for eighteen years and this place, these people—it's all brand new.

I'm proud to say Austin is my home for the next four years, and I'm sure I'll look back from the future and think of all the great opportunities and friendships that started at goto.
